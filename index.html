§ 01 · Premise

From chat assistants and deep-research products to artifact-producing research agents.

Language models have moved beyond chat assistants. They now sit inside systems that browse the literature, write and execute code, run simulations, and produce research artifacts such as figures, reports, and even draft papers. We refer to this emerging class of systems as AI Scientists: language model agents that carry out substantial parts of the research loop, rather than answering one question at a time.

The pace of change accelerated sharply in late 2025 and early 2026. Karpathy's autoresearch, Google's AI co-scientist, FutureHouse's Kosmos, Biomni, AI-Researcher, MiroFlow, OAgents, SciMaster, and Tongyi DeepResearch all appeared in roughly twelve months — alongside new benchmarks (ResearchGym, ResearchClawBench, Scientist-Bench, LAB-Bench) that ask whether frontier agents can do real research rather than only answer benchmark questions.

Yet the literature is fragmented across NLP, ML, agent, and domain-specific venues, using different vocabularies for the same concepts. The community lacks a shared map of what an AI Scientist actually does, how its capabilities should be measured, and how NLP should contribute responsibly.

This tutorial answers those questions by organizing AI Scientist research around the end-to-end research workflow: a six-stage pipeline that recurs across recent systems regardless of domain.

§ 02 · Outline

Seven blocks along the research workflow.

From motivating successes and failures, through foundations of the research loop, to evaluation, reliability, safety, and open problems.

Module 01 · 15 min

Welcome & Motivating Cases

Successes and failures from recent AI Scientist systems: a citation-hallucinating drafting agent; a coding agent that cannot reproduce its own results; a biomedical agent that proposes a CRISPR screen; a multi-agent co-scientist that ranks novel hypotheses.

Module 02 · 30 min

Foundations of the Research Loop

The common pipeline that recurs across systems: problem → grounding → ideation → planning → execution → analysis → writing → iteration. Three design axes: autonomy level, loop closure, and artifact scope.

Module 03 · 30 min

Idea Generation & Grounding

Chain-of-Ideas, tournament-style hypothesis evolution, monitor-based RAG, and the meta-problem: which ideas should an agent actually try? Learned idea comparators, citation grounding, and faithfulness.

Module 04 · 35 min

Coding, Experiments & Tool Use

Research coding as a distinct problem. SARE-style adaptation, Biomni-style unified action spaces (150+ tools, 59 databases), orchestration patterns (CodeAct, planner+executor, debate, evolve-and-select), and a live walkthrough of a minimal AI Scientist loop.

Module 05 · 25 min

Analysis, Writing & Artifacts

Turning raw experimental outputs into research artifacts: figure and report generation, hierarchical multi-stage paper writing for long-manuscript consistency, LM-critic self-review, and recurring failure modes (overclaiming, broken figure-text consistency).

Module 06 · 25 min

Evaluation & Reliability

Multi-dimensional evaluation: task success, reliability, reproducibility, citation faithfulness, novelty. ResearchGym, ResearchClawBench, Scientist-Bench, LAB-Bench, BMMR, LabUtopia, OSDA Agent — and the empirical capability–reliability gap.

Module 07 · 20 min

Safety, Oversight & Open Problems

Hallucinated citations, overclaiming, authorship and attribution, unsafe tool execution in wet-lab settings. Human-in-the-loop research workflows (DeepScientist, LabOS), AI-XR co-scientists, and a curated list of open problems on which NLP can have immediate impact.

The question moves beyond whether an agent can answer a research question. It becomes whether an agent can run the research itself — ground in prior work, plan, code, experiment, analyze, write, and revise — and how we should evaluate the result.

§ 06 · Reading List

A curated path through the AI Scientist landscape.

Organized by tutorial workflow — pre-readings recommended before attending, additional readings for broader background. Links will be added as releases become public.

1.Foundations & Landscape

Surveys and landscape papers that define the AI Scientist space, and the work that popularized the term "auto research." Read these first to anchor the rest of the tutorial.

Pre-reading

  • A Survey of AI Scientists.Paper arXiv:2510.23045 (v4)
  • autoresearch — an automated research loop for overnight ML experiments.Code Andrej Karpathy

Additional reading

  • Agentic AI for Scientific Discovery: A Survey of Progress, Challenges, and Future Directions.Paper Survey of agentic AI for science across chemistry, materials, biology, and physics
  • Autonomous Agents for Scientific Discovery: Orchestrating Scientists, Language, Code, and Physics.Paper Orchestration of language, code, and physics in autonomous discovery
  • The Rise and Potential of Large Language Model Based Agents: A Survey.Paper Zhiheng Xi et al.

2.End-to-End AI Scientist Systems

Representative end-to-end systems that span ideation, coding, experimentation, and writing — in NLP/ML, biomedicine, chemistry, materials, and astronomy.

Pre-reading

  • Biomni: A General-Purpose Biomedical AI Agent.Paper Kexin Huang, Yuanhao Qu, Henry C. Cousins, William A. Johnson, Di Yin, Mihir Shah, Denny Zhou, Russ B. Altman, Mengdi Wang, Le Cong
  • Towards an AI Co-Scientist.Paper Juraj Gottweis, Wei-Hung Weng, Alexander Daryin, Tao Tu, Anil Palepu, Petar Sirkovic, et al. (Google DeepMind)

Additional reading

  • AI-Researcher: Autonomous Scientific Innovation (with Scientist-Bench).Paper HKU Data Science Lab
  • Kosmos: An AI Scientist for Autonomous Discovery.Paper FutureHouse
  • SciMaster: X-Master as Foundation — Can We Lead on Humanity's Last Exam?Paper SciMaster Team
  • Tongyi DeepResearch: A New Era of Open-Source AI Researchers.Blog Alibaba Tongyi Lab
  • CRISPR-GPT for Agentic Automation of Gene-Editing Experiments.Paper Yuanhao Qu, Kaixuan Huang, Ming Yin, Kanghong Zhan, Dyllan Liu, et al. — Nature Biomedical Engineering
  • OSDA Agent: De Novo Design of Organic Structure Directing Agents with LLMs.Paper Chemistry / materials AI Scientist

3.Coding, Tool Use & Orchestration

Research coding is a distinct problem from generic software engineering. These papers cover research coding agents, tool environments, and orchestration patterns (single-agent CodeAct, planner+executor, debate, evolve-and-select).

Pre-reading

  • Self-Adapting Agents for Automating Research Coding Workflows (SARE).Paper Balaji Dinesh Gangireddi, Aniketh Garikaparthi, Manasi Patwardhan, Arman Cohan — ICLR 2026 RSI
  • MiroFlow: A Consistent Agent Framework with Reproducible Performance.Paper MiroFlow Team

Additional reading

  • OAgents: An Empirical Study of Building Effective Agents.Paper OAgents Team
  • WebThinker: Empowering Large Reasoning Models with Deep Research Capability.Paper Deep research with reasoning models
  • Eigen-1: Adaptive Multi-Agent Refinement with Monitor-Based RAG for Scientific Reasoning.Paper Wanghan Xu et al.
  • SWE-bench: Can Language Models Resolve Real-World GitHub Issues?Paper Carlos E. Jimenez, John Yang, Alexander Wettig, Shunyu Yao, Kexin Pei, Ofir Press, Karthik Narasimhan

4.Ideation, Ranking & Oversight

Idea generation is no longer the bottleneck. Recent work has shown that idea evaluation — ranking, novelty assessment, oversight — is becoming its own technical subproblem.

Pre-reading

  • Forecasting Research Success Through Learned Comparison of Scientific Ideas.Paper Kushal Tirumala, Chenglei Si, Bohan Zhang, Bohan Wang, Mengjiao Yang, Kathleen M. Carley, Yejin Choi, Graham Neubig, Diyi Yang

Additional reading

  • Chain-of-Ideas (from AI-Researcher): challenge → existing methods → motivation → proposed method → technical details → expected outcomes.Paper HKU Data Science Lab

5.Evaluation & Reliability Benchmarks

Final-answer accuracy is not enough. These benchmarks measure end-to-end research capability, reliability, reproducibility, citation faithfulness, and novelty — in NLP/ML, biomedicine, chemistry, materials, and embodied scientific settings.

Pre-reading

  • ResearchGym: Evaluating Language Model Agents on Real-World AI Research.Paper Aniketh Garikaparthi, Manasi Patwardhan, Arman Cohan
  • ResearchClawBench: From Re-Discovery to New-Discovery.Repo InternScience

Additional reading

  • Scientist-Bench (from AI-Researcher).Paper HKU Data Science Lab
  • LAB-Bench (DbQA, SeqQA) and HLE-Biomedical, as used by Biomni.Paper Biomedical reasoning and database querying benchmarks
  • BMMR: A Large-Scale Bilingual Multimodal Multi-Discipline Reasoning Dataset.Paper Multimodal multi-discipline reasoning
  • LabUtopia: High-Fidelity Simulation and Hierarchical Benchmark for Scientific Embodied Agents.Paper Rui Li, Zixuan Hu, Wenxi Qu, Jinouwen Zhang, Zhenfei Yin, Sha Zhang, et al.

6.Safety, Oversight & Human-in-the-Loop

The unique risk profile of AI Scientists: hallucinated citations, overclaiming, authorship and attribution, and unsafe tool execution in wet-lab settings. Plus design patterns for human-in-the-loop research workflows.

Pre-reading

  • LabOS: The AI-XR Co-Scientist That Sees and Works With Humans.Paper Le Cong, David Smerkous, Xiaotong Wang, Di Yin, Zaixi Zhang, et al.
  • DeepScientist — Local-First Persistent Research Workflows.Repo ResearAI

Additional reading

  • SafeToolBench: A Benchmark for Pre-Execution Tool-Safety in Language Agents.Paper Tian Xia et al.
  • OASIS: Open Agents Social Interaction Simulations on One Million Agents.Paper Ziyi Yang, Zaibin Zhang, Zirui Zheng, Yuxian Jiang, Ziyue Gan, Zhiyu Wang, Zhenfei Yin
